Understanding Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is one of the most common and serious types of cancer. It begins in the lungs and can spread to other parts of the body. Early detection significantly improves treatment outcomes and survival rates.

Health Awareness

Quit Smoking

The most important step to prevent lung cancer. Seek help from cessation programs and support groups.

Avoid Secondhand Smoke

Stay away from areas where people smoke regularly. Secondhand smoke contains over 7,000 chemicals.

Regular Screenings

If you're at high risk, get regular CT scans for early detection. Early diagnosis improves survival rates significantly.

Healthy Lifestyle

Exercise regularly and eat a diet rich in fruits and vegetables. Physical activity strengthens respiratory muscles.

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water to keep lung mucus thin. Proper hydration helps maintain healthy respiratory function.

Get Vaccinated

Protect your lungs with flu and pneumonia vaccines. Vaccination reduces risk of respiratory infections.
