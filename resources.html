‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about lung health and screening

Who should get screened for lung cancer?

Annual lung cancer screening is recommended for adults aged 50-80 who have a 20 pack-year smoking history and currently smoke or have quit within the past 15 years. Consult your doctor if you have risk factors like family history or occupational exposure.

What is a low-dose CT scan?

Low-dose CT (LDCT) is a specialized imaging technique that uses significantly less radiation than conventional CT scans. It creates detailed images of the lungs and can detect small nodules that may indicate early-stage cancer.

Can lung cancer be cured if caught early?

Yes, when detected at an early stage (Stage I), the 5-year survival rate for lung cancer can be as high as 80-90%. This is why early detection through screening is so crucial. Unfortunately, most cases are diagnosed at later stages when symptoms appear.

How accurate is AI-based lung cancer detection?

Modern AI systems can achieve accuracy rates of 90-95% in detecting lung nodules and abnormalities. However, AI is designed to assist, not replace, medical professionals. All AI findings should be reviewed and confirmed by qualified radiologists.

What are the main risk factors for lung cancer?

The primary risk factors include smoking (responsible for 80-85% of cases), secondhand smoke exposure, radon gas, asbestos and other occupational exposures, air pollution, family history of lung cancer, and previous radiation therapy to the chest.
